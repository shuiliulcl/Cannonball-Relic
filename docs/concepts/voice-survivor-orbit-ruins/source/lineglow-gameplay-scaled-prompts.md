## Enemy Density Scale Sheet

Original production concept sheet for a Canvas 2D top-down survivor roguelite called Voice Survivor: Cannonball Relic. Focus on enemy token sizes, density, and activity readability for the current gameplay. Dark neutral background with three small arena strips or clusters showing sparse, mid, and dense combat density. No labels, no readable text, no letters, no numbers, no watermark.

Style: dark stone arena, luminous contour line art, self-glow color accents, compact top-down tokens, high gameplay contrast, small runtime readability, cozy cosmic archaeology, ancient signal magic. Less brass metal, more dark bodies with colored glow rims and internal glowing cracks/cores.

Design seven enemy families at runtime-appropriate scale. Common enemies should look like 26-32 px tokens: fast runner with orange eye slash, triangular pouncer with amber outline, cyan ranged lens drone, green repeater leaf-wheel signal. Larger/special enemies should look like 34-42 px tokens: cracked orange brute, violet silencer/gravity cantor with ring larger than body, red target relic core with danger ring.

Show the same enemies repeated in activity clusters: early sparse 8-12 units, mid 18-24 units, dense 30+ units. Units should remain individually readable in the dense cluster. Use distinct silhouette plus glow color, not detailed armor. Use simple motion cues: runner short streak, pouncer forward triangle, ranged beam cone, repeater rotating small ticks, silencer violet area ring, target red pulse. Avoid black blobs that vanish into the background, avoid uniform brass armor, avoid hollow transparent interiors, avoid thin limbs, hair, smoke, fur, or wispy edges.
